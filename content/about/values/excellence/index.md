Excellence is one of my core values because it is the bridge between intention and impact. It is not enough to have good ideas; excellence means doing the work with care, attention, and consistency until the outcome matches the ambition.

I first heard those words from my neighbor, Uncle Shenoy. While helping him fix his computer, he looked up and said, “You have excellent technical skills.” That moment stayed with me because it was an honest recognition of craft — not just completion, but competence delivered when it mattered.

At the same time, I have wrestled with communication. Conversations with managers have often exposed gaps in how I express ideas, share progress, and align expectations. I have been clear with the work I can do, yet I have not always been clear enough in the way I say it.

That tension is what drives my pursuit of excellence in communication. As editor in chief of Lead AI Launchpad, I use my blog, newsletter, and podcast to sharpen how I write, speak, and connect with others. Every post, every episode, and every newsletter is a chance to turn a raw idea into something precise, useful, and trustworthy.

Excellence for me is both technical mastery and the relentless improvement of how I communicate that mastery to the world.
